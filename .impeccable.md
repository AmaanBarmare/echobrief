## Design Context

### Users
Operators, founders, PMs, consultants, and team leads running back-to-back meetings across languages — India-first today, global tomorrow. They're time-poor, platform-fluent, and have tried Otter / Fireflies / Grain. They want a product that feels familiar and modern, not one that makes them learn a new visual vocabulary to find their action items.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **calm, modern, confident.**

- **Calm** — lots of whitespace, no visual theatrics, nothing shouting for attention.
- **Modern** — clean type, clean lines, familiar SaaS patterns done well. The reference set is Linear, Vercel, Notion, Superhuman.
- **Confident** — short, direct copy. No adjectives like "AI-powered", "revolutionary", "next-gen". No editorial cosplay ("dispatch", "filed", "the desk"). Just say what it does.

Emotional goal: the quiet relief of an app that clearly respects your time.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Clean modern SaaS.** Plain product copy. One workhorse typeface. Orange ember as a subtle brand accent on the primary CTA and active states only — not a motif hammered onto every card. Standard layouts (hero, feature grid with varied sizes, step flow, FAQ, CTA) executed well — not reinvented.

**Anti-references (explicit):**
- No editorial / newspaper voice. No "Volume 01", no "Nº 0142", no "filed from the control room", no small-caps mono kickers over every section.
- No oversized display serif italics announcing each heading.
- No gradient text, no side-stripe card borders (skill absolute bans).
- No icon-above-heading identical card grids (skill-banned pattern).
- No generic AI palette (cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, neon accents).

**Theme:** both light and dark are first-class. Light = warm paper (not pure white). Dark = warm charcoal (not pure black). Ember orange stays consistent across both, used sparingly.

**Fonts (single-family approach, Linear/Vercel style):**
- **Switzer** (Fontshare) — the one workhorse. Covers body, UI, and headings via weight + size, 300–800.
- **JetBrains Mono** — only where monospace is actually needed (code blocks, transcript timestamps). Never as a decorative "technical vibe" shorthand.
- **Noto Sans Devanagari / Tamil** — via `:lang()` fallback only, not loaded globally.

**Palette (OKLCH, warm-tinted neutrals, both themes):**
- `ember` — oklch(62% 0.18 40). Primary CTA, active state, recording indicator. Used rarely.
- Light: paper `oklch(97% 0.008 60)`, surface `oklch(99% 0.004 65)`, ink `oklch(18% 0.02 40)`, ink-mid `oklch(42% 0.015 42)`, ink-soft `oklch(58% 0.01 45)`, rule `oklch(90% 0.01 50)`.
- Dark: char `oklch(15% 0.01 40)`, surface `oklch(20% 0.012 40)`, bone `oklch(94% 0.008 60)`.
- No pure black or white. All neutrals carry a barely-perceptible warm tint.

### Design Principles
1. **Familiar beats clever.** Standard SaaS patterns (hero, feature grid, steps, FAQ, CTA, sidebar + header + list) done well. Users shouldn't have to decode the UI.
2. **Copy is plain.** "Meeting summaries that actually make sense" beats "Every meeting, filed as a dispatch." No metaphors. Short sentences. India-aware where it matters (Hindi, Tamil, Hinglish, WhatsApp) but not performatively so.
3. **Ember is rare.** Orange appears on the primary CTA, the active nav item, the recording dot. That's it. Everywhere else is warm neutral.
4. **Space and weight, not decoration.** Hierarchy comes from size + weight + whitespace, not colored bars, underlines, or label strips.
5. **One typeface.** Switzer carries everything. Resist the urge to pair a display serif — that urge is what produced the editorial detour.
